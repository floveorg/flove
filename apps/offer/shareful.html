✦ Summary

💡 Who — who will use this, and at what distance?

Name the people behind your clause: who is invited (close · near · all), and what kind of trust the lending implies.

💡 What — what are you lending and what use does it allow?

Read the verb of your offer: lend, share, swap, hand-down. The framing tells the receiver how to relate to the thing.

💡 Where — where does the thing live, and how does it come back?

Spell out the place and the return path: where it is, after-use return, on-request return. Boundaries make sharing breathable.

💡 Why — why this offering, in this form?

Ask what kind of relation your lending invites — careful, generous, playful, conditional. The shape of the agreement is half of the answer.
